ENGR 480 Project Proposal

Powered Wheelchair Safety Innovation

Company: U4Uc - Unique Innovations for Unique Challenges

The U4Uc mission is to bring together innovation partners, such as university design teams, and innovation advocates, such as powered wheelchair users, to solve unique challenges experienced by the advocates. U4Uc empowers advocates experiencing unique challenges that may have gone unresolved or unrecognized. U4Uc empowers partners to understand real challenges and team with those most aware of these challenges. Together they can learn understanding and altruism while learning that together they matter and are powerful.

Contact

Kayla Latterell, Project Manager, kayla@secretlab.com

Project Type

This project will consist of research and development of a prototype.

Application/Market Area Description

There are approximately 2.7 million wheelchair users in the United States, many of whom use a powered wheelchair. The powered wheelchair market is also projected to grow significantly given the aging US population. The current US market size for wheelchairs is approximately 3 billion dollars. Cost effective safety enhancements are expected to form a significant segment of this market.

Problem to be Solved

Powered wheelchair users need to exercise extreme caution near certain obstacles: static obstacles (e.g., stairwells, ramp edges) and dynamic obstacles (e.g., pets). This increases tenfold when an environmental factor, such as icy ramps during winter, is added among other obstacles. The burden of safety is currently borne almost exclusively by the chair user as they navigate near these obstacles. Similarly, if a collision or an accident occurs, there are currently minimal safeguards to prevent the user from unintentionally engaging the joystick on the chair’s motor control system and worsening the accident. 

One such incident happened with the innovation advocate associated with this project, Shane Burcaw. As Shane used a familiar ramp to exit a home, the wheelchair did not have enough friction to stay on course during peak winter months. Due to slippage, he was jerked forward, resulting in pushing the joystick forward to accelerate. The wheelchair tipped over, crushing Shane underneath. In addition, one of the straps that secured him to the chair failed. To rescue Shane from this accident, his dog assisted him by barking for help. The wheelchair was too heavy to be lifted alone by the first person to arrive. The problem to be solved in this project is to prevent accidents from occurring due to static and dynamic objects, as well as mitigate the severity of an accident through appropriate monitoring of the motor control system.

Requirements for the Design

The powered wheelchair safety system must provide detection system of common indoor or outdoor obstacles around the wheelchair, provide appropriate notification to the user, provide an interface to the chair user, and prevent the motor control system from responding to commands that could result in an accident or exacerbate an accident that has already occurred.  The prototype must be able to perform better than a human user and it must minimize any false-positive detection of obstacles or collisions. The design must be suitable for retrofit onto several chair models across multiple manufacturers. The design must also comply with any relevant regulations and standards (e.g. IEC 61508, WC19, and ISO 7176).

Deliverables

Students will provide a functional prototype as well as its supporting documentation: manufacturing drawings, solid models, electrical schematics, and design verification data collected using a modern powered wheelchair (to be provided by the sponsor and project advocate).

Sponsor Resources

U4Uc is committed to the success of this project and will provide access to a powered wheelchair, technical guidance and expertise by a guest innovator, project management tools (i.e., a custom developed smart phone app), and access to a chair user who is the advocate for the team’s solution.

Guest Innovator - Heta Diwan

Heta has her master’s degree in Robotics, specializing in autonomous vehicles. Her thesis was based on developing an add-on device for wheelchairs using the ROS framework on Linux platform. The thesis consisted of applying SLAM and path planning using C++ and Python to develop obstacle detection and autonomous navigation systems. She can assist in providing technical guidance and expertise regarding the tools she has used and other resources.

Challenge Advocate - Shane Burcaw

Shane was born with a neuromuscular disease called Spinal Muscular Atrophy that requires him to use an electric wheelchair. He is the author of several award-winning books, the president of a nonprofit organization called Laughing At My Nightmare, and a renowned public speaker who has performed across the country at places such as Harvard University, The University of Florida, The University of Connecticut, Princeton University, and many more.
