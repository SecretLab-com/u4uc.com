U4Uc: Promoting Independence, Altruism, and Education

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the mission?

Our mission is to bring together innovators and people with unique challenges. U4Uc empowers people experiencing Unique Challenges that may have gone unresolved or unrecognized. U4Uc empowers Innovators to understand real challenges and team with those most aware of these challenges. Together they can learn understanding and altruism while learning that together they MATTER and are POWERFUL.

When will this happen?

We are doing a trial run right now to work out any usability issues. We hope to scale it up to a cohort of innovators and advocates by next year with our first university partner. Contact us if you want to be part of a future cohort.

Can I see students talk about their experience? Where can I see more examples of challenges being solved?

We are doing a trial run right now and hope to scale it up to a cohort of innovators and advocates by next year with our first university partner. Once we create the experiences, the videos will be posted on our website.

What is the scope of the differences and challenges?

Any challenge selected is experienced by a person or group of people, distinct from the majority (e.g., by ability, location, race, access, other) traditionally marginalized or denied support and resources. A challenge innovation must potentially benefit a person or population in a meaningful way providing inclusion into mainstream activities that were otherwise denied due to something beyond one’s control. In addition, any innovation must be realistically deliverable within the time limits of a given cohort.

The video tells the Why but what is the Who and the How? Who is the audience? Do they work directly with the university?

Bright Foundation is partnering with university staff to create this innovation experience. In addition, business will support the mission with resources. Students, referred to as the Innovation Partner, at the university will join this effort as part of their course work. They offer technical or adaptive ideas and exploration of design but have limited understanding of the challenge. A person or group, referred to as the Innovation Advocate, joins the team having direct experience with the challenge and eventually ensures that the solution fits the challenge. The Innovation Partner and the Innovation Advocate collaborate equally with each other. Eventually this will be done at the middle and high school level.  

Is it also done for high school?

This is currently only at the university level. Eventually this will be done at the middle and high school level.

Is this done independently or as a class, club, or department?

Currently, students, referred to as the Innovation Partner, at the university will join this effort as part of their course work.  

How do participants benefit from this experience?

U4UC uses real world examples to teach the complete world of entrepreneurship and Innovation: client selection, client management, team management, agile development, continuous improvement, pitch, funding, diversity, monetization, and most importantly self-worth.
